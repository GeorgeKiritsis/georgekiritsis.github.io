Defended my diploma thesis Ikaros & Skyris at the Hellenic Air Force Academy (grade 100/100), receiving the Best Thesis of Class 2026 distinction.

I obtained my BSc in Computer Science from the Hellenic Air Force Academy in 2026, graduating top of my class with the Best Thesis of Class 2026 distinction (100/100) for the Computer Science Department. My undergraduate research at the 360 Training Squadron, supervised by Assoc. Prof. Panagiotis Karampelas, produced an operationally deployed end-to-end flight-data acquisition and debriefing platform, comprising a custom STM32-based flight-data computer (Ikaros), a C++/OpenGL 3D replay and visualization engine (Skyris), and a phase-aware Isolation Forest pipeline for automated anomaly detection and flight grading.

My work sits at the intersection of software engineering, robotics, computer graphics, embedded systems, operating systems, aerospace machine learning, and real-time analytics for pilot training.

Selected Projects

Skyris Engine

C++20 OpenGL ECS Real-time ML

Custom C++20/OpenGL 3D engine for flight replay and debriefing analytics. EnTT ECS architecture, physically based rendering, quadtree LOD terrain from NASA SRTM, OpenStreetMap layer, Jolt physics, deterministic replay with LERP/SLERP interpolation, phase-aware Isolation Forest anomaly detection. Deployed operationally at the 360 Training Squadron of the Hellenic Air Force Academy.

Deep-dive → GitHub (coming soon)

Ikaros Flight Computer

STM32 Embedded C PCB Design Sensor Fusion

Custom flight-data recorder for the Tecnam P2002-JF trainer aircraft. STM32F405 ARM Cortex-M4, BMI088 IMU, LIS2MDL magnetometer, BMP280 barometer, NEO-6M GPS, microSD logging over SPI/I2C. Bare-metal embedded C firmware with Mahony AHRS attitude estimation, per-channel Kalman filtering, FatFs-based persistent CSV logging at 100 Hz.

Deep-dive → GitHub (coming soon)

FLARES – Flight Logs for Anomaly Research and Evaluation Suite

Python JSBSim Benchmark Open-source

Open-source synthetic-to-real benchmark for anomaly detection in general-aviation pilot training. JSBSim-based 6-DOF flight dynamics with five physically-grounded anomaly categories across three severity tiers. Synthetic-to-real validation against the NGAFID-MC real Cessna 172 dataset (28,935 flights, 31,177 hours).
